Kimchi: The Soul of Korea

Explore what kimchi is, how it evolved, why it's loved worldwide, and how to make a simple version at home.

Introduction

Baechu kimchi, made with napa cabbage, is one of Korea's most iconic fermented foods. Seasoning is carefully spread between the leaves, and the flavor becomes deeper and tangier as it ferments.

Kimchi is commonly served as a side dish, but it is also used in stews, fried rice, pancakes, and more. That flexibility is one reason it remains central in Korean cuisine.

History & Types

Kimchi has developed over centuries and differs by region, season, and family tradition. Some kimchi is spicy and rich, while other types are mild, watery, and refreshing.

Popular varieties include dongchimi (watery radish kimchi), oi-sobagi (stuffed cucumber kimchi), and pa-kimchi (green onion kimchi). Each has its own texture, aroma, and level of spiciness.

Health Benefits

Kimchi is often associated with probiotics (lactic acid bacteria) because fermentation supports the growth of beneficial microbes. It also provides plant fiber and various vitamins from vegetables and seasonings.

Many people enjoy kimchi as a flavorful side dish that can fit into a balanced diet.

How to Make Kimchi (Simple Guide)

Follow these five steps to make a basic baechu kimchi at home.

1
Salt the Napa Cabbage

Cut the napa cabbage into quarters and rub salt generously between the leaves. Let it rest for 1‚Äì2 hours until the leaves soften and release water.

2
Rinse and Drain

Rinse the salted cabbage under cold running water two or three times to remove the excess salt. Squeeze gently and let it drain in a colander for about 30 minutes.

3
Make the Seasoning Paste

Mix gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), minced garlic, grated ginger, and fish sauce (or salted shrimp) together in a bowl until you have a thick, fragrant red paste.

4
Season the Cabbage

Put on gloves and spread the paste evenly between every leaf of the cabbage. Make sure each layer is fully coated so the flavor develops evenly during fermentation.

5
Pack and Ferment
